What is Bhagavad Gita?

The *Bhagavad Gita*, often referred to simply as the *Gita*, is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the ancient Indian epic, the *Mahabharata*. It is a philosophical dialogue between Prince Arjuna, a warrior, and Lord Krishna, his charioteer, set on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.

Krishna imparts profound spiritual wisdom, explaining the concepts of dharma (duty), karma (action), and moksha (liberation). The *Gita* teaches selfless action, focusing on the eternal soul, and outlines three paths to spiritual realization: karma yoga, bhakti yoga, and jnana yoga.

The *Bhagavad Gita* serves as a guide for living a balanced, ethical, and purposeful life.

Significance of Bhagavad Gita

The *Bhagavad Gita* holds immense significance as a spiritual and philosophical guide. It addresses fundamental questions about life, duty, and purpose, offering wisdom that transcends religious boundaries.

The *Gita* emphasizes selfless action, without attachment to outcomes, fostering personal growth. It presents three paths to spiritual realization: karma yoga, bhakti yoga, and jnana yoga.

Its teachings on the eternal soul (atman) encourage readers to focus on inner peace and spiritual growth, providing timeless wisdom for leading a purposeful life.
